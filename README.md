# BrewJobs
### Brand Identity, Marketing Strategy & Full Stack Website

> You Set the Vibe. We Bring the Quality Coffee.

---

## Overview

BrewJobs is a local coffee shop in Maypearl, Texas. This project was unique from the start because BrewJobs and The Twisted Pearl, a modern microvenue and event space, share the same building separated by a barn door. Two businesses, one address, zero margin for brand confusion.

I built the BrewJobs brand completely from scratch, starting with creative direction, logo design, and brand strategy before a single line of code was written. From there I built and launched the website, manage their social media presence, and continue to serve as their ongoing marketing person and brand strategist.

---

## The Challenge

- Two distinct businesses sharing one building needed two completely distinct brand identities
- BrewJobs needed to feel welcoming, local, and laid back while standing on its own separate from The Twisted Pearl next door
- The website needed to be easy to maintain and update without developer involvement for day to day content
- Social media growth needed to happen fast for a brand new business in a small town with no existing audience

## Brand Identity

Starting from zero, I developed the full BrewJobs brand identity including:

- **Brand Strategy** — Defined the brand positioning, voice, and target audience. BrewJobs needed to feel like the kind of place where everyone belongs, whether you're grabbing a quick cup or staying all afternoon.
- **Logo Design** — Created the logo and visual identity from scratch, building a look that felt local, approachable, and distinct from The Twisted Pearl sharing the same building.
- **Creative Direction** — Established the visual language, color palette, typography, and tone of voice that carries across the website, social media, and all marketing materials.

## Technical Highlights

### Reusable Component Architecture
The BrewJobs website was built with a strong focus on reusable components, making the codebase clean, consistent, and easy to maintain and expand as the business grows. With a blog coming soon and ongoing content updates, a solid component foundation was essential.

### Online Ordering Integration
Rather than building a custom ordering system, I integrated BrewJobs with Clover for online ordering, giving the client a reliable and familiar tool for managing orders while keeping the website experience seamless for customers.

### Menu
The site features a full menu that can be updated and maintained without developer involvement, keeping the client in control of their own content day to day.

## Marketing & Social Media

### The Facebook Plot Twist
Going into the social media launch I expected Instagram to be the primary growth channel. Visual product, small town community, it seemed like the obvious fit. The data told a different story.

Facebook took off instead, growing from 0 to 600 followers in 4 months through organic content and community engagement alone. No paid ads. Just showing up consistently with content that resonated with the Maypearl community.

The lesson: let the data tell you where your audience actually is, then meet them there. I adapted the strategy accordingly and leaned into Facebook as the primary growth channel.

### Business Impact
In their first 4 months of operation BrewJobs generated **$40,000 in sales**, a direct result of the brand, marketing strategy, and community engagement work done from day one.
